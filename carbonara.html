Spaghetti Carbonara

Prep Time: 15 - 20 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Total Time: 30 - 35 minutes

Introduction

I am a BIG pasta lover, and one of my all time fave pasta dishes to eat is spaghetti carbonara. I just love the cheesy creaminess and the slight meaty notes you get from the pancetta style bacon that usually accompanies it. It's an all time Italian classic that I just had to include in my online portfolio piece.

Ingredients

100g pancetta

50g pecorino cheese

50g parmesan

3 large eggs

350g spaghetti

2 plump peeled and whole garlic cloves

50g unslted butter

sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

1. Put a large saucepan of water on to boil.

2. Finely chop the 100g pancetta, having first removed any rind. Finely grate 50g pecorina cheese and 50g parmesan and mix them together.

3. Beat the 3 large eggs in a medium bowl and season with a little freshly grated black pepper. Set aside.

4. Add 1 tsp salt to the boiling water, add 350g spaghetti and when the water comes back to boil, cook at a constant simmer, with lid covering it, for 10 minutes or until al dente.

5. Squash 2 peeled plump garlic cloves with the blade of a knife to bruise them.

6. While the spaghetti is cooking,, fry the pancetta with the garlic. Drop 50g of unsalted butter into a large frying pan or wok and, as soon as the butter melts, tip in the pancetta and garlic.

7. Leave to cook on a medium heat for about 5 minutes, stirring often, until the pancetta is golden and crisp. The garlic has used all its flavour so discard it with a slotted spoon.

8. Keep the heat under the pancetta on low. When the pasta is ready, lift it from the water with tongs or a pasta fork and put it in the frying pan with the pancetta. Don't throw the pasta water away.

9. Mix most of the cheese in with the eggs, keeping a small handful back for sprinkling over later.

10. Take the pan of spaghetti and pancetta off the heat. Quickly pour in the eggs and cheese. Using tongs or a long fork, lift up the spaghetti so it mixes easily with the egg mixture, which thickens but doesn't scramble and everything coated.

11. Add extra pasta water to keep it saucy. You don't wnt it wet, just moist. Season with a little salt if need be.

12. Use a long pronged fork to twist the pasta onto the serving plate or bowl. Serve immediately with a sprinking of cheese and a grating of black pepper. If the dish gets dry, splash in more hot pasta water to revive the moisture.
