Halloumi Fries

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 10 minutes

Total Minutes: 20 minutes

Introduction

Halloumi Fries are a great alternative to normal fries and they are a personal fave of mine to nibble on before a hearty meal. This recipe uses a spicy yoghurt for the dipping sauce but my preferred dipping sauce that I highly recommend is sweet chilli. Enjoy these cheesy, chewy delights to your heart's content!

Ingredients

170g pot of Greek yoghurt

1 lemon

1 tbsp rose harissa

3 tbsp za'atar

75g plain flour

2 x 250g blocks of halloumi

oil

mint

Instructions

1. Mix the yoghurt with the lemon zest and some seasoning, then swirl through the harissa so that you have pockets of hot and cool in the dip.

2. On a plate, stir the za'atar into the flour, then roll the halloumi in the mixture so it's evenly coated. Heat the oil in a shallow, heavy-bottomed pan or a casserole dish until 180 degrees C on a cooking thermometer, or a piece of bread browns in 20 seconds. Working in batches, carefully lower the halloumi into the oil and cook for 2 minutes until crisp and golden, then drain on kitchen paper.

3. Sprinkle over the mint and za'atar, and serve with the lemon wedges and spicy yoghurt for dipping.
