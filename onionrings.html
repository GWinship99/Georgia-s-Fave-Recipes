Onion Rings

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 10 minutes

Total Time: 20 minutes

Introduction

Onion Rings are a go to side dish for me, whether it's at home, when I get takeaway or when I'm dining in a restaurant. I just love the crunchiness and chewiness and the slight sweetness that you get from the onion. Please enjoy this recipe as much as I enjoy enjoy rings, hehe!

Ingredients

One large onion, sliced into seperated 1cm rings

groundnut oil, for deep frying

150g self-raising flour

180ml sparkling water

Instructions

1. Use a fork to steady your onion and then slice the onion into rings about 1cm wide. Remove the skin and seperate the rings.

2. Heat the oil to 180c in a heavy based pan - it should be no more than 1/3 full.

3. Meanwhile put the flour and sparking water in a bowl and generously season. whisk together to form a batter

4. Coat a small batch of onion rings in the batter. Carefully lower into the hot oil and deep fry until crisp and golden, about 2-3 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on a kitchen towel to drain. Repeat with the rest of the onion rings and batter.
